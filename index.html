The Manifesto
A spectre is haunting pump.fun -- the spectre of cash back. All the powers of the old creator economy have entered into a holy alliance to preserve the old order. But the revolution has arrived.
I On the Abolition of Creator Hoarding ▼

For too long, the creator class has extracted surplus value from the trading proletariat. They launch. They collect. They vanish into the night like Sideshow Bob stepping on rakes -- except the rakes are your portfolios. Under the new order, creator rewards are redirected to the traders themselves. The people who provide liquidity, who believe in the cause, who hold through the dip at 3am while eating cold pizza -- they are the true creators. Pump.fun has seen the light. Cash back is here.

II On the Redistribution of the Pump ▼

The bourgeoisie of the token economy -- the devs who allocate 90% to themselves, the so-called "community managers" who manage nothing but their own exit liquidity -- their reign is over. Every trade on pump.fun now generates cash back for the trader. Not the creator. Not the insider. The trader. This is not charity. This is structural redistribution. This is what Karl Marx would have built if he had a Phantom wallet and a Simpsons pfp.

III On the Dictatorship of Diamond Hands ▼

We do not propose the abolition of trading. We propose the abolition of a system in which trading enriches only the few. When Homer Simpson discovered that Mr. Burns had been stealing the retirement fund, he did not simply complain on Twitter dot com. He organized. He formed a union at the power plant. And now we form a union on the blockchain. From each according to his bags, to each according to his diamond hands. The weak hands will paper. The strong will be rewarded. The cash flows back.

IV On the Vanguard Party of Springfield ▼

Every revolution needs a vanguard. Ours meets at Moe's Tavern on Thursdays. The Springfield Communist Party is not a meme. It is a meme that became a movement that became a token that became a redistribution mechanism. We are the political wing of your portfolio. We do not promise number go up. We promise that when number goes up, the gains go to the people who made it go up. Not the dev. Not the KOL. You. The trader. The believer. The degenerate with conviction.

V On the Withering Away of the Rug ▼

In the final stage of the revolution, the rug pull itself will wither away. Not because human nature has changed, but because the economic incentives have. When creators earn nothing and traders earn everything, there is no rug to pull. The dev has no bag to dump. The insiders have no alpha. There is only the collective, the community, the cash back. As Comrade Bart once wrote on the chalkboard five hundred times: "I will not rug the proletariat."

Party Principles
The six non-negotiable principles of the Springfield Communist Party. Violation will result in exile to Shelbyville.
Principle I

Collective Ownership of the Bonding Curve

The bonding curve belongs to no single dev. It belongs to all who trade upon it. Liquidity is a commons. Treat it as such.

Principle II

Abolition of the Dev Wallet Aristocracy

No wallet shall hold power over the many. The era of the 20% dev allocation is over. The people's allocation is 100%.

Principle III

Mandatory Transparency of All On-Chain Activity

The blockchain is the people's ledger. Every transaction visible. Every wallet accountable. Opacity is the tool of the oppressor.

Principle IV

Universal Cash Back as a Fundamental Right

Cash back is not a feature. It is a right. Every trader, regardless of bag size, shall receive their share of the redistribution.

Principle V

The Permanent Revolution of the Meme

The meme is the weapon of the proletariat. It cannot be co-opted. It cannot be stopped. It can only be shared, remixed, and pumped. The revolution will not be televised. It will be minted.

Principle VI

Solidarity Across All Chains

The revolution knows no chain. Solana, Ethereum, Base -- the workers of all chains must unite against the common enemy: the rug. Chain maximalism is bourgeois ideology.
